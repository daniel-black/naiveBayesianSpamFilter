<td bgcolor="#282C3B"><font color=white size=2 face="arial, helvetica, geneva">When <i>Street Fighter vs. X-Men</i> came out, I was overjoyed at the prospect of pitting Ken and Ryu against some of my favorite comic book characters. Unfortunately, I could only play it in the arcade, since neither the PlayStation or Saturn could handle the tag-team elements, which involved switching characters during gameplay. However, Sega won my loyalty forever with a pretty crazy idea. They provided a RAM pack for the Saturn in Japan that gave the system the muscle it needed. I then modified my Saturn with a switch on the back so that you could access Japanese or American games. Not only did it work, but it was an arcade-perfect translation. People often bandy around the term "arcade perfect," but this game truly merited it. <br>
<i>--Guthwulf</i><br></font>
